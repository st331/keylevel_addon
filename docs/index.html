WARCRAFT LOGS KEY % FOR YOUR M+ APPLICANTS — AT THE KEY YOU'RE RUNNING

Reading the table: every number is a Key % — the per-key-level DPS percentile from a report's Damage Done tab, as it stood when the run happened (so gear inflation later in the season doesn't distort old runs). 91b 84a 87m = best · average · median. In the middle column that's across the dungeons they logged at that level; in the right column it's across their runs of your dungeon — at your level, a higher one (counts at least as much), or one below. Click a row for the dungeon × level breakdown — every percentile there links to the exact report it came from. Only keys within ±4 of yours are considered.

Every run is judged by the role it was played in: T tank and D dps runs are ranked on damage, H healer runs on healing (HPS Key %). Players get one chip per role that holds any of their top keys (their highest-scored run of each dungeon — up to 8), ordered by how many it holds; ties go to whichever role they've played most recently. A stray off-role run earns no chip. The first, solid chip is the view shown — click a dimmed chip to judge them in that role instead. Numbers are frozen as of each run's day. A report page opened long ago can show a stale cached Key % — the linked report is your audit trail.

Colors are Warcraft Logs tiers · a player with no rows simply doesn’t log · looked-up characters are cached in your browser for an hour (Shift-click Look up for fresh data) · addon & source
